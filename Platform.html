    Project

    Reseach was commissioned by City of Helsinki. The primary aim of thesis was to identify guest boat service providers’ needs for a new platform service. The research was topical for the commissioner, as it provided information of the guest harbor services and service providers´ wishes for an ongoing service platform development project. The subject of the study supports the development objectives of the City of Helsinki and the Helsinki Marine project.

    Information was collected by interviewing experts and boaters. The interviews were utilized in the theoretical framework, which focused on boating, platform services and service design. Because of commissioner’s request for a clear delimitation of the subject and for its own interest, guest boat operators were selected as the target group and the scope of the work was limited to definition of needs. The target group for the research was guest boat service providers in Helsinki. The target group consisted of companies, associations and a public sector service provider.

    The main results of the research consisted of the operators' business descriptions, benefits of the platform service and needs for the platform service. The results showed that the guest boat service providers´ profitability is impacted by businesses in harbor area. Interviewees wished the platform functions and features would ease their daily work and purchase process for customers. Common platform service was believed to increase the market demand and to provide added value by measuring quality of the services and enabling local and supplemental services.

    The results showed that ports with high utilization rate were less likely to benefit from the shared platform service compared to ports with lower usage rates. In conclusion, the key needs to the platform service were increased utilization rate of guest harbor boat capacity, promoting the sales, facilitating the daily work and improving the accessibility and discoverability.
